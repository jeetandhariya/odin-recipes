Surati Undhiyu

Description

If you are a fan of mixed vegetable recipes from the Indian cuisine, this recipe post is sure for you. And even if you are not, then post trying this classic Gujarati recipe, I’m sure you would be. This is a super delicious Surti Undhiyu Recipe, which does not take much time as compared to the traditional one. This Undhiyu is a Gujarati style mixed vegetable dish, which is prepared majorly with the seasonal fresh produce that the winters in India bring along. You can’t really not try it, as if you do, you’ll miss out on something tasty as well as wholesome.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup surti papdi (flat green beans)
  • 1 cup small brinjals (eggplant)
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 cup peanuts, roasted and crushed
  • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/2 cup jaggery, grated
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix all the vegetables with turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and asafoetida. Once they splutter, add the marinated vegetables and mix well.
  3. Add grated coconut, crushed peanuts, tamarind pulp, jaggery, and water. Mix well and cover the pot.
  4. Cook on low heat for about 30-40 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add lemon juice and fresh coriander leaves. Mix well and serve hot with puris or rotis.
  6. Enjoy your delicious Surati Undhiyu!
