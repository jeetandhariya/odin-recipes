Farali Pattice

Description

Farali pattice is an easy snack of patties made with potatoes and stuffed with a sweet filling of coconut, nuts and raisins. This dish is from the Gujarati cuisine that is made during fast or upvas. I often make these pattice during Navratri and also on regular days.

Ingredients

  • 2 large potatoes
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Boil the potatoes until soft. Peel and mash them.
  2. In a pan, heat some oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add grated coconut, peanuts, raisins, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Take a small portion of the mashed potato and flatten it in your palm. Place a spoonful of the coconut mixture in the center and fold the potato over it to form a patty.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and fry the patties until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with green chutney or ketchup.
